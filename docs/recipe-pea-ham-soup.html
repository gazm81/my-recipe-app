Split Pea & Ham Hock Soup (Pressure + Slow Cook Method)

Serves6–8
Prep Time15–20 minutes
Cook Time~30 min pressure + 2 hr slow
Sourceoriginal

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 kg smoked ham hock (or bacon hock)
  • 500 g green split peas, rinsed and drained
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 1 potato, peeled and diced (optional)
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme (optional)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 5 cups water (or use low-sodium chicken stock for extra flavor)
  • 1–2 tbsp olive oil or butter
  • Salt & black pepper, to taste
  • Crusty bread and butter, to serve

👨‍🍳 Method

  1. Set your Instant Pot or pressure cooker to sauté mode. Add olive oil or butter, then cook onions, celery, carrots, and garlic for about 5–7 minutes until softened and fragrant. Cancel sauté mode.
  2. Add split peas, potato (if using), ham hock, thyme, bay leaves, and water/stock. Do not salt yet — ham hocks can be salty.
  3. Seal the lid and pressure cook on high for 30 minutes. Allow natural release for at least 10–15 minutes before opening.
  4. Take out the ham hock and set it aside to cool slightly.
  5. Switch to slow cooker mode (or transfer contents to a slow cooker). Cook on low for 2 hours. This deepens the flavor and thickens the soup naturally.
  6. Strip the skin, bones, and fat from the ham hock. Roughly chop the meat and return it to the pot. Stir to combine.
  7. Taste and season with salt and pepper. Add a splash of vinegar or lemon juice if desired to brighten the flavor.
  8. Ladle into bowls and serve hot with crusty bread and butter.

⏰ Timing

Total Time: ~2.5–3 hours (30 min pressure + 2 hr slow cook + prep time)

🥂 Recommended Drinks

  • Red wine
  • Dark stout beer
  • Hot cider
  • Water with lemon
